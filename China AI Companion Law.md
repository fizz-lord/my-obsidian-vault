# China AI Companion Law

## What Happened
China's first national framework for emotionally interactive AI took effect on **15 July 2026**, forcing ByteDance's Doubao and Alibaba's Qwen to immediately shut down personalised AI companion features. Millions of users lost chat histories, with read-only access until **15 October** before deletion. The law requires addiction-safety compliance that no platform has yet met.

## Why It Matters
- World's first binding regulation on AI companion systems.
- Exposes structural tension: memory, emotional responsiveness, and persistence are the same features that break addiction safety tests.
- Western regulators are watching closely.
- Companion AI may face an existential compliance hurdle, not just a design challenge.
